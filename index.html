ABOUT
Ganghwadae Island is where I spent the most defining years of my childhood.

For my teenage self boarding here, Ganghwa was merely a monotonous rural island, and every weekend, I yearned to escape to the bustling noise of Seoul.

Later in my 20s, I moved to the capital to pursue music and played in bands, losing my own rhythm amidst the city's frantic velocity.

When I felt I could endure it no longer, I packed up everything and fled back here to Ganghwa.

Back on the island, I met and recorded the deep resonance of the evening bell from Jeondeungsa Temple blending with the hum of grass insects, the Angelus bell of Onsu-ri Cathedral, and the shifting faces of the sea shaped by the ebb and flow of the tide.

This process has become a quiet window to look back upon my weary soul.
